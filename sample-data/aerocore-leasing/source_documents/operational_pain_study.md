# AeroCore Operational Audit & Customer Reviews

---

## 📋 CASE STUDY: Manual Reconciliation Friction in Agricultural Drone Fleets
*   **Prepared by:** Operational Excellence Group, LLC
*   **Target Assessment:** AeroCore Billing & Dispatch Reconciliations
*   **Evaluation Date:** March 12, 2026

### Executive Summary
AeroCore's usage-based billing process is heavily bottle-necked by manual data transcribing. Currently, the company leases hardware under a structure that limits flight hours to 40 hours per month, charging an overage fee of $85.00/hour. However, the telemetry tracking is disconnected from the main billing software.

### Key Pain Points & Measured Metrics:
1.  **Manual Overage Audit Bottleneck:** 
    *   *Process:* Every month, Billing Administrator Sarah Jenkins must manually extract flight minute logs from the telemetry SQL server, sum them up, convert them to hours, calculate the hours exceeded, and manually write billing adjustment line items on client invoices.
    *   *Metric:* This process takes an average of **26 minutes per invoice**. Across 250+ monthly active leases, this consumes **108 hours of manual labor per month**, causing billing cycles to drag 5–7 days past the start of the month.
2.  **Data Transcription Errors:**
    *   *Process:* Repair technicians calculate carbon arm repairs, replacement motor costs, and engineering hours on paper repair logs. These logs are transcribed manually into billing records.
    *   *Metric:* Audit results showed a **4.8% transcription error rate**, resulting in approximately **$12,400 in lost billing revenue** in Q4 2025 due to missed parts charges and transposed digits in serial numbers.
